Title: Mobile and Edge NPUs — On-Device AI

The proliferation of NPUs in mobile and edge devices represents one of the most consequential shifts in AI deployment. Moving inference from the cloud to the device offers compelling advantages: reduced latency, enhanced privacy, offline operation, and lower per-query cost for device manufacturers. However, edge NPUs must operate within severe power budgets, typically 1-10 watts, demanding extreme efficiency.

Apple's Neural Engine is the most influential mobile NPU lineage. The A11 Neural Engine (2017) delivered 600 billion operations per second (GOPS) from a 2-core design. Each successive generation has compounded gains: the A12 Neural Engine (2018) reached 5 TOPS from an 8-core design, the A14 (2020) hit 11 TOPS, the A15 (2021) achieved 15.8 TOPS, the A16 (2022) reached 17 TOPS, and the A17 Pro (2023) hit 35 TOPS. The A18 (2024) pushes to 38 TOPS with architectural improvements targeting on-device large language model inference. Apple's Neural Engine is tightly coupled to the Core ML framework and the ANE (Apple Neural Engine) compiler, which automatically partitions model layers across CPU, GPU, and Neural Engine based on operation type and available bandwidth.

Qualcomm's Hexagon NPU, integrated into Snapdragon SoCs, serves the Android ecosystem. The Snapdragon 8 Gen 1 (2022) achieved 26 TOPS, the 8 Gen 2 (2022) reached 34 TOPS, and the 8 Gen 3 (2023) hit 45 TOPS of INT4 performance. Qualcomm's AI Engine Direct SDK and the Neural Processing SDK provide developers with tools to deploy quantized models on Hexagon hardware. A notable feature of Qualcomm's approach is the Hexagon Tensor Processor, a fused architecture that combines a vector DSP, scalar core, and tensor accelerator in a single programmable unit, offering more flexibility than pure systolic array designs.

MediaTek's APU (AI Processing Unit) appears in Dimensity SoCs targeting mid-range and premium Android devices. The Dimensity 9300 (2023) features an NPU with 35 TOPS performance and supports running models up to 7 billion parameters on-device. MediaTek emphasizes power efficiency in its APU designs, with the Dimensity 9300 NPU achieving 7B-parameter LLM inference at under 2 watts.

Beyond smartphones, edge NPUs appear in a rapidly expanding range of form factors. Automotive NPUs process sensor fusion data for advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) and autonomous driving. NVIDIA's Drive Orin SoC delivers 254 TOPS for automotive AI, while Mobileye's EyeQ Ultra targets 176 TOPS at 10 watts for ADAS workloads. Industrial cameras, smart speakers, wearables, and IoT sensors increasingly incorporate microNPUs with sub-100-milliwatt power envelopes. Arm's Ethos-U55 and U65 are microNPU IP blocks designed for Cortex-M class microcontrollers, enabling machine learning inference on devices with as little as 64 KB of SRAM.

The software ecosystem for edge NPUs has matured significantly. TensorFlow Lite, ONNX Runtime, PyTorch Mobile, and vendor-specific runtimes like Apple's Core ML and Qualcomm's SNPE all provide model optimization pipelines including quantization, pruning, and operator fusion. The challenge of fragmentation — dozens of incompatible NPU architectures with different ISAs and memory hierarchies — has driven standardization efforts. The ONNX format and the MLCommons MLPerf Mobile benchmark suite help compare NPU performance across vendors on standardized workloads.
